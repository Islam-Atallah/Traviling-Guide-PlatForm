Hanging Garends of Babylon

The Hanging Gardens of Babylon were one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World listed by Hellenic culture. It was built in the ancient city of Babylon and its current location is close to the city of Hilla, Babil Governorate, Iraq ....

The Hanging Gardens of Babylon were attributed to the Babylonian king Nebuchadnezzar II, who ruled between 562 and 605 BC. He stated that the reason for its construction was to satisfy his wife, the Queen of Babylon, who was the daughter of one of the leaders of the armies that allied with his father, who exerted great efforts in conquering the Assyrians. And her name was Amites Al-Maidonia, who missed living in the hills of Persia and hated living in the flat land of Babylon. Therefore, Nebuchadnezzar decided to inhabit it in a building on top of a hill made by men, in the form of gardens with terraces
